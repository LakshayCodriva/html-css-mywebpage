Jantar Mantar

Jantar Mantar is an astronomical observatory located in Delhi, India. It was built by Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur in the early 18th century. The observatory consists of a collection of architectural astronomical instruments that were used to measure time, predict eclipses, and track celestial bodies. Jantar Mantar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its unique design and historical significance in the field of astronomy.

Major Instruments at Jantar Mantar There are 13 instruments at the site like:

1. Samrat Yantra
A giant triangular sundial Used to measure time with high precision Can calculate time accurate to seconds
2. Jai Prakash Yantra
Helps determine celestial coordinates Used to track the position of stars
3. Ram Yantra
Measures altitude and azimuth of celestial bodies
4. Misra Yantra
Combines multiple observational techniques

Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort located in Delhi, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century and served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. The fort is made of red sandstone and features impressive architecture, including massive walls, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens. The Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is an important symbol of India's rich history and culture.

Important Structures Inside Red Fort:

1. Diwan-i-Aam
Public audience hall where the emperor met common people.
2. Diwan-i-Khas
Private hall for meetings with ministers and nobles.
3. Rang Mahal
Residence of royal women.
4. Moti Masjid
A small marble mosque inside the complex.

Ratings: 4.3 stars

Address: Netaji Subhash Marg, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Delhi 110006, India

India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial located in Delhi, India. It was built in honor of the Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. The monument is designed in the style of the Arc de Triomphe in Paris and stands at a height of 42 meters. India Gate is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of national pride, often visited by locals and tourists alike to pay their respects to the fallen soldiers.

National Importance India Gate is one of the most important patriotic landmarks in India. It serves as:

  • A war memorial
  • A ceremonial site
  • A public gathering place
  • A tourist attraction
National celebrations and parades often take place nearby, especially the Republic Day Parade along Kartavya Path.

Ratings: 4.9 stars

Address: Kartavya Path, India Gate, New Delhi, Delhi 110001
